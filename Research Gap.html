Research Gap




In Sri Lanka anyone has not ever created an IoT-based rainfall prediction system, according to previous research articles. They've merely constructed a rainfall forecasting system based on historical data. The goal of this research is to develop an IoT-based rainfall forecasting and monitoring system. Despite the fact that researchers throughout the world have created IoT-based rainfall prediction systems, these systems are not viable for Sri Lanka according to the climatic of the country characteristics and topography. We should really have a need of a sophisticated system, especially in a heavily populated area like Colombo since Sri Lankans have suffered floods.

Past weather data are collected from the Meteorological Department and from the Internet for this research, and after pre-processing, the rainfall is predicted using the Long-Short Term Memory (LSTM) model and Prophet Algorithm. The most accurate model is chosen from among these prediction models, and a prediction is made. Both LSTM Algorithm and Prophet models are faster and smoother. And, predicted weather data parameters that are Rainfall, Humidity, Temperature and Pressure and is visualized in a web application.

DHT11 Temperature and Humidity sensor, BMP/BME280 Barometric Pressure Sensor, and Water Level Sensor will be used to develop an IoT device with IoT sensors that collect real-time weather data on temperature, rainfall, humidity, and air pressure. The real-time data are displayed in a web application every 5 seconds.

When considering to the past research papers in Sri Lanka, most of researchers had done flood forecasting systems for the main basins in Sri Lanka. But some past researchers had researched the Food mitigation and flood risk measuring applications.

There is no comparable research project in Sri Lanka that compares to this one in terms of past research. All other programs collect data by processing past data, however, our approach is based on real-time IoT data. When we use foreign flood detection methods, they do not match our country's climate. As a result, we had to create a climate-control system for our country. The Colombo neighborhood was chosen for this project since it is frequently flooded throughout the city.
